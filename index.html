SkinGuardian’s Focus: Why Skin Cancer?

Skin Cancer is the most common cancer in the United States and worldwide.
It can affect anyone, regardless of skin color.

Skin Cancer Risk Factors:

  • UV light exposure
  • Skin type
  • Family / personal history of skin cancer
  • Weak immune system
  • Age

Skin Cancer is the cancer you can see. That’s why skin exams, both at home and with a dermatologist, are especially vital.

Early detection saves lives. Learning what to look for on your own skin gives you the power to detect cancer early when it’s easiest to cure, before it can become dangerous, disfiguring or deadly.

That is why I am working on: SkinGuardian

An AI-powered digital companion for the early detection of skin cancer

The Vision Behind SkinGuardian

  • Classify skin lesions
  • Generate medically accurate clinical insights
  • Support self-conducted skin exams, especially when access to dermatologists is limited
  • Help in the prevention of skin cancer through personalized recommendations and lifestyle factors tracking

Ultimately, support early detection—when treatment is most effective.

Scope and Tools

Since my vision for SkinGuardian was too ambitious for the limited time span that I had, I decided to focus on its main functionality: Classifying Skin Lesions.

To reach this goal, this was my toolset:

Next Steps

• Deploy “SkinGuardian” as a web/mobile app that provides accessible skin lesion diagnostics for both patients and clinicians, making early detection easier and more widespread.

• Leverage fine-tuned large language models (LLMs) with Retrieval-Augmented Generation to generate more accurate clinical diagnoses. This next iteration—SkinGuardian V1—could integrate comprehensive medical literature to provide context-aware advice.

• Train the model with auxiliary tasks (e.g., risk-factor assessment, lesion segmentation) and keep iterating on functionalities to enhance diagnostic accuracy and user experience.
